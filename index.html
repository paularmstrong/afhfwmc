Bug 13240 - Consider replacing <time> with <data>

On October 29th, 2011, the <time> element was removed from HTML5 and replaced with <data>, citing that <time> was too ambiguous and wasn't getting enough traction.1

  1. http://www.w3.org/Bugs/Public/show_bug.cgi?id=13240

There are several use cases for <time>:
A. Easier styling of dates and times from CSS.
B. A way to mark up the publication date/time for an article (e.g. for conversion to Atom).
C. A way to mark up machine-readable times and dates for use in Microformats or microdata.

Use cases A and B do not seem to have much traction. Use case C applies to more than just dates, and the lack of solution for stuff outside dates and times is being problematic to many communities.

–Ian Hickson, WhatWG Editor

  1. http://www.w3.org/Bugs/Public/show_bug.cgi?id=13240#c1
  • I doubt any of HTML5 elements have much traction.1
  • Of all elements in HTML5, time is the one that is least ambiguous1
  • <time> *does* express something more useful than just <data> or <meta> in that it tells the UA that its contents are a datetime. <data> would just tell the UA "there is something here" and would be semantically equivalent to <span>2
